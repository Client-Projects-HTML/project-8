Regular maintenance is the key to extending your commercial roof's lifespan and avoiding costly repairs. Here are the top 5 maintenance tasks every building owner should perform.

1. Schedule Regular Inspections

Professional inspections should be conducted at least twice a year - ideally in spring and fall. Additionally, always inspect your roof after severe weather events. Look for:

  • Cracks, blisters, or splits in the membrane
  • Loose or damaged flashing
  • Standing water or ponding
  • Debris accumulation
  • Signs of vegetation growth

2. Keep Gutters and Drains Clear

Clogged gutters and drains can cause water to pool on your roof, leading to leaks and structural damage. Regularly remove leaves, debris, and other obstructions. Consider installing leaf guards for added protection.

3. Address Small Issues Immediately

A small crack or loose flashing today can become a major leak tomorrow. Address minor issues as soon . Regular maintenance is as they're discovered far more cost-effective than emergency repairs.

4. Trim Overhanging Trees

Tree branches can damage roofing materials and deposit debris that clogs drains. Keep trees trimmed back from your roofline to prevent physical damage and reduce debris accumulation.

5. Document Everything

Keep detailed records of all inspections, repairs, and maintenance activities. This documentation helps track your roof's condition over time and can be valuable for warranty claims or when selling the property.
